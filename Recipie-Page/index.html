This was a reicipe I picked up after I returned home from the field, and was stuffed full of rich, calorie-laden camp food. This was something much more wholesome than the stuff I was eating on a regular basis.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp peanut oil
  • 1 tbsp cajun seasoning
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 tsp hot pepper sauce
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 diced onion
  • 1 diced green pepper
  • 2 diced stalks of celery
  • 3 minced cloves of garlic
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken; cubed
  • 10 oz andouille sausage, cut into rounds
  • 1 can italian crushed tomatoes
  • 2 1/2 cups chicken broth

Directions

  • Prep: 30min
  • Cook: 45min
  • Total: 1hr, 15min
  1. In a large pot, heat the peanut oil over medium heat. Season the chicken and sausage with cajun seasoning. Saute sausages until brown. Remove and set aside. Add another tbsp of peanut oil and saute the chicken over medium heat until browned. Then remove and set aside the chicken as well.
  2. In the same pot, saute onion, bell pepper, celery and garlic until tender. Stir in crushed tomatoes, and season with red pepper, black pepper, salt, hot pepper sauce, Worcestershire sauce and file powder. Stir in chicken and sausage. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the rice and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes, or until liquid is absorbed.
